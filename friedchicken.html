Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken wings
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together flour, cornstarch, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Coat chicken wings in the flour mixture, shaking off any excess.
  4. Carefully place the coated chicken wings into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and cooked through, about 10-12 minutes.
  5. Remove the fried chicken wings from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  6. For the sauce, mix together 1/2 cup gochujang (Korean chili paste), 1/4 cup soy sauce, 1/4 cup honey, 2 tablespoons rice vinegar, and 2 cloves minced garlic in a saucepan.
  7. Heat the sauce over medium heat until it thickens slightly, stirring occasionally.
  8. Toss the fried chicken wings in the sauce until evenly coated.
  9. Serve hot, garnished with sesame seeds and chopped green onions, if desired.
